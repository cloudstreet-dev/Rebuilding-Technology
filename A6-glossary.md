# Appendix A6: Glossary

Technical terms used throughout this book, with definitions accessible to readers rebuilding civilization.

---

## A

**AC (Alternating Current)**: Electric current that periodically reverses direction. Standard for power distribution. See DC for contrast.

**Addendum**: The height of a gear tooth above the pitch circle.

**Alloy**: A mixture of metals, or a metal mixed with other elements. Bronze (copper + tin) and steel (iron + carbon) are alloys.

**Alternator**: A generator that produces alternating current (AC).

**Ammeter**: An instrument that measures electric current in amperes.

**Ampere (amp, A)**: The unit of electric current. One ampere equals one coulomb of charge per second.

**Annealing**: Heat treatment that softens metal, making it easier to work. The opposite of hardening.

**Anode**: The positive electrode. In batteries, the terminal that releases electrons; in electroplating, the metal being dissolved.

**Anvil**: A heavy block (traditionally iron or steel) on which metal is shaped by hammering.

**Arc welding**: Welding process using an electric arc to melt metals.

**Armature**: The rotating part of a DC motor or generator, containing the windings.

**AWG (American Wire Gauge)**: Standard system for measuring wire diameter in North America. Higher numbers mean smaller wire.

---

## B

**Babbitt**: A soft alloy (tin, antimony, copper) used for bearings. Named after Isaac Babbitt.

**Backlash**: The clearance or play between mating gears or threads.

**Ball bearing**: A bearing using spherical balls between inner and outer races to reduce friction.

**Bellows**: A device that produces a stream of air, traditionally used to feed forges. Made from leather or fabric.

**Bevel gear**: A gear with teeth cut on a cone, used to transmit power between shafts at an angle.

**Bit (tool)**: The cutting part of a drill or other tool.

**Blast furnace**: A tall furnace for smelting iron ore using a continuous blast of air.

**Bloomery**: A primitive furnace for making iron directly from ore, producing a "bloom" of iron.

**Bore**: 1. The interior diameter of a tube or cylinder. 2. To make a hole by removing material.

**Brace**: A hand tool for rotating drill bits. Also called a brace and bit.

**Brass**: An alloy of copper and zinc.

**Brazing**: Joining metals using a filler metal that melts above 450°C but below the base metal's melting point.

**Bronze**: An alloy of copper and tin.

**Brushes**: Carbon or graphite blocks that conduct electricity to the rotating part of a motor or generator.

**Bushing**: A plain bearing—a cylindrical sleeve in which a shaft rotates.

---

## C

**Calibration**: Adjusting an instrument to read correctly against a known standard.

**Cam**: A rotating or sliding piece that transforms rotary motion into linear motion.

**Capacitance**: The ability to store electric charge. Measured in farads.

**Capacitor**: An electrical component that stores energy in an electric field between two conductors.

**Carburizing**: Adding carbon to the surface of iron or steel to harden it.

**Casting**: Forming metal by pouring molten material into a mold.

**Catalyst**: A substance that speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumed.

**Cathode**: The negative electrode. In batteries, the terminal that receives electrons.

**Center punch**: A pointed tool for making small indentations to guide a drill bit.

**Charcoal**: Carbon made by heating wood without air. Used for fuel, filtration, and metallurgy.

**Chuck**: A device for holding a workpiece or tool, typically with adjustable jaws.

**Clearance**: The gap between two parts.

**Clutch**: A device for connecting and disconnecting a driving and driven part.

**Coke**: A fuel made by heating coal without air. Purer and hotter-burning than coal.

**Commutator**: A rotary switch in DC motors/generators that reverses current direction as the armature rotates.

**Compound**: A substance made of two or more elements chemically bonded.

**Compression**: 1. Squeezing force. 2. The compression stroke in an engine.

**Condenser**: 1. A device for cooling vapor back to liquid. 2. Old term for capacitor.

**Conductivity**: The ability to conduct heat or electricity.

**Coupling**: A device for connecting two shafts.

**Crucible**: A container that can withstand high temperatures, used for melting metals.

**Current**: The flow of electric charge, measured in amperes.

---

## D

**DC (Direct Current)**: Electric current that flows in one direction only. Batteries produce DC.

**Dedendum**: The depth of a gear tooth below the pitch circle.

**Die**: 1. A tool for cutting external threads. 2. A mold for stamping or forming.

**Diode**: An electronic component that allows current to flow in one direction only.

**Distillation**: Separating liquids by boiling and condensing. Used to purify water, make alcohol, refine petroleum.

**Drawknife**: A woodworking tool with a blade between two handles, pulled toward the user.

**Drill press**: A machine tool for making holes, with the drill mounted on a vertical column.

**Ductility**: The ability of a material to be drawn into wire without breaking.

**Dwell**: A period during which a cam follower remains stationary.

**Dynamo**: An older term for a DC generator.

---

## E

**Efficiency**: The ratio of useful output to total input, usually expressed as a percentage.

**Electrode**: A conductor through which electricity enters or leaves.

**Electrolyte**: A substance (usually liquid) that conducts electricity through the movement of ions.

**Electromagnet**: A magnet created by passing electric current through a coil of wire.

**EMF (Electromotive Force)**: Voltage—the "pressure" that pushes electric current.

**Escapement**: A mechanism in clocks that controls the release of energy from the power source.

---

## F

**Face width**: The width of a gear, measured parallel to its axis.

**Farad**: The unit of capacitance.

**Fatigue**: Weakening of material from repeated stress cycles, leading to failure.

**Feed**: The rate of advance of a cutting tool into the workpiece.

**Ferrous**: Containing iron.

**File**: A hardened steel tool with cutting ridges for shaping metal.

**Fillet**: A rounded internal corner.

**Fit**: The relationship between mating parts—tight fit, loose fit, etc.

**Flange**: A projecting rim or edge.

**Flux**: 1. In soldering/welding, a substance that promotes metal bonding by removing oxides. 2. In magnetics, the strength of a magnetic field.

**Flywheel**: A heavy wheel that stores rotational energy and smooths power delivery.

**Forging**: Shaping metal by heating and hammering.

**Frequency**: The number of cycles per second, measured in hertz (Hz).

**Friction**: Resistance to motion between surfaces in contact.

**Fuller**: A blacksmith's tool for spreading metal.

---

## G

**Galvanizing**: Coating iron or steel with zinc to prevent rust.

**Gasket**: A sealing material between two surfaces.

**Gauge**: 1. A measuring instrument. 2. A standard size (wire gauge, sheet gauge).

**Gear ratio**: The ratio of teeth between driving and driven gears.

**Generator**: A machine that converts mechanical energy to electrical energy.

**Gib**: An adjustable strip for taking up wear in a slideway.

**Governor**: A device that automatically controls speed.

**Graphite**: A form of carbon used as a lubricant and in electrodes.

**Grease**: A semi-solid lubricant made from oil and a thickener.

**Grinding**: Removing material with an abrasive wheel.

**Ground (electrical)**: A reference point for electrical potential; connection to earth.

---

## H

**Hardening**: Heat treatment that makes steel harder but more brittle.

**Hardy**: A chisel that fits in the anvil's hardy hole.

**Heat treatment**: Controlled heating and cooling of metal to change its properties.

**Helical gear**: A gear with teeth cut at an angle to the axis.

**Henry**: The unit of inductance.

**Hertz (Hz)**: The unit of frequency; one cycle per second.

**Hob**: A cutting tool for generating gear teeth.

**Horsepower**: A unit of power. 1 HP ≈ 746 watts.

**Hub**: The central part of a wheel or gear.

**Hydraulics**: The use of liquids under pressure to transmit power.

---

## I

**Idle**: Running without load.

**Idler**: A gear or pulley that transmits motion without changing speed ratio.

**Impedance**: Opposition to alternating current, combining resistance and reactance.

**Inductance**: The property of a conductor that opposes changes in current.

**Inductor**: A coil that stores energy in a magnetic field.

**Induction**: 1. Generating voltage by changing magnetic field. 2. Magnetizing by proximity.

**Ingot**: A mass of metal cast in a convenient shape for storage or further processing.

**Insulation**: Material that prevents the flow of electricity or heat.

**Involute**: The curve used for gear tooth profiles; traced by a point on a string unwinding from a circle.

---

## J

**Jig**: A device that holds and guides a tool during machining.

**Journal**: The part of a shaft that rotates in a bearing.

---

## K

**Kerf**: The width of a cut made by a saw.

**Key**: A piece that locks a gear or pulley to a shaft.

**Keyway**: A slot in a shaft or hub to receive a key.

**Kiln**: A furnace for firing ceramics, making lime, or drying materials.

**Kinetic energy**: Energy of motion.

---

## L

**Lap**: 1. To polish with fine abrasive. 2. A tool used for lapping.

**Lathe**: A machine tool that rotates the workpiece against a cutting tool.

**Lead (pronounced "leed")**: The distance a screw advances in one revolution.

**Lead (pronounced "led")**: A soft, dense metal (Pb).

**Linkage**: A system of connected levers or bars.

**Lubricant**: A substance that reduces friction between moving surfaces.

---

## M

**Mandrel**: A shaft on which work is mounted for machining or assembly.

**Mild steel**: Low-carbon steel, easily worked but not hardenable.

**Mill (machine)**: A machine tool that removes material with a rotating cutter.

**Module**: In gears, the pitch diameter divided by the number of teeth (metric system).

**Moment**: A force times its distance from a pivot point; causes rotation.

**Motor**: A machine that converts electrical energy to mechanical energy.

---

## N

**Needle bearing**: A rolling bearing using thin cylindrical rollers.

**Neutral**: 1. In electricity, the return conductor. 2. In gearing, not driving or driven.

**Normalizing**: Heat treatment that refines grain structure in steel.

---

## O

**Ohm (Ω)**: The unit of electrical resistance.

**O-ring**: A circular rubber seal with round cross-section.

**Oscillation**: Back-and-forth motion.

**Oxidation**: Chemical reaction with oxygen. Burning and rusting are oxidation.

---

## P

**Parallel**: Side by side, never meeting. Also: electrical connection where components share the same voltage.

**Pawl**: A pivoting lever that engages ratchet teeth.

**Pitch**: 1. The distance between adjacent threads or gear teeth. 2. The angle of a propeller blade.

**Pitch circle**: The theoretical circle on which gear calculations are based.

**Plain bearing**: A bearing without rolling elements; a bushing.

**Plating**: Coating one metal with another, usually by electrochemistry.

**Pneumatics**: The use of compressed air to transmit power.

**Pole**: 1. A magnetic north or south pole. 2. A terminal of a switch.

**Potentiometer**: A variable resistor.

**Power**: The rate of doing work. Measured in watts or horsepower.

**Pressure angle**: In gears, the angle between the line of force and the tangent to the pitch circle.

**Pulley**: A wheel with a groove for a belt or rope.

---

## Q

**Quench**: Rapid cooling of hot metal, usually in water or oil, to harden it.

---

## R

**Rack**: A straight bar with gear teeth.

**Ratchet**: A mechanism allowing motion in one direction only.

**Reactance**: Opposition to alternating current from inductors and capacitors.

**Reamer**: A cutting tool for finishing holes to precise size.

**Reciprocating**: Moving back and forth in a straight line.

**Rectifier**: A device that converts AC to DC.

**Relay**: An electrically controlled switch.

**Reluctance**: Opposition to magnetic flux in a material.

**Resistance**: Opposition to electric current, measured in ohms.

**Resistor**: A component that provides electrical resistance.

**Refractory**: Material that withstands high temperatures without melting.

**Rotor**: The rotating part of a motor, generator, or turbine.

**RPM**: Revolutions per minute.

---

## S

**SAE**: Society of Automotive Engineers; establishes many standards.

**Saponification**: Chemical reaction that makes soap from fats and alkali.

**Scale**: 1. Iron oxide that forms on heated steel. 2. A measuring instrument.

**Scribe**: A pointed tool for marking metal.

**Seal**: A device that prevents leakage.

**Series**: Electrical connection where current flows through one component then the next.

**Shaper**: A machine tool that makes flat surfaces with a reciprocating cutting tool.

**Shear**: Force that causes sliding between layers of material.

**Shim**: A thin piece used to fill a gap or adjust alignment.

**Slag**: The non-metallic waste product from smelting.

**Slake**: To add water to quicklime, producing slaked lime.

**Smelting**: Extracting metal from ore by heating with a reducing agent.

**Solder**: A low-melting alloy used to join metals.

**Solenoid**: A coil of wire that produces a magnetic field when energized; often with a movable core.

**Spindle**: A rotating shaft, especially one that holds a tool or workpiece.

**Spur gear**: A gear with teeth parallel to its axis.

**Stator**: The stationary part of a motor or generator.

**Stroke**: The distance a piston travels.

---

## T

**Tallow**: Animal fat, rendered for use in candles, soap, and lubricants.

**Tap**: A tool for cutting internal threads.

**Taper**: Gradual change in diameter along a length.

**Temper**: 1. To reheat hardened steel to reduce brittleness. 2. The hardness/toughness of steel.

**Tensile strength**: Maximum pulling force a material can withstand before breaking.

**Thread pitch**: The distance between adjacent threads.

**Tolerance**: The acceptable range of variation in a dimension.

**Tongs**: Hand tools for gripping hot metal.

**Torque**: Rotational force, measured in pound-feet or newton-meters.

**TPI**: Threads per inch.

**Transformer**: A device that changes AC voltage levels using magnetic coupling.

**Transistor**: A semiconductor device for amplifying or switching electronic signals.

**Trunnion**: A pin or pivot on which something rotates.

**Tuyere**: The nozzle through which air enters a furnace.

---

## U

**Undercut**: A groove or relief below a surface.

**Universal joint**: A coupling that transmits rotation between shafts at an angle.

---

## V

**Vacuum tube**: An electronic device using electron flow through a vacuum.

**Valve**: A device that controls fluid flow.

**Viscosity**: A fluid's resistance to flow; thickness.

**Volt**: The unit of electrical potential (voltage).

**Voltage**: Electrical "pressure" that causes current to flow.

**Vulcanization**: Chemical process that hardens rubber by adding sulfur.

---

## W

**Watt**: The unit of power; one joule per second.

**Weld**: To join metals by fusion, usually with heat.

**Wick**: Absorbent material that draws up liquid by capillary action.

**Winding**: Coils of wire in a motor, generator, or transformer.

**Worm gear**: A gear system with a screw (worm) driving a wheel.

**Wrought iron**: Nearly pure iron, soft and workable, containing slag fibers.

---

## Y

**Yield strength**: The stress at which a material begins to deform permanently.

**Yoke**: A frame or bracket, often U-shaped.

---

## Z

**Zero**: The reference point on a scale.
